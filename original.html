Myths & truths

"Will I cluck like a chicken?"

Tap a card. Most of what people fear about hypnosis comes from stage shows, not clinics.

"I'll lose control."
Tap for the truth
Myth

You stay aware and in charge the whole time. You can open your eyes, speak, or stop whenever you choose. A hypnotherapist is a guide, not a puppeteer.

"I could get stuck in hypnosis."
Tap for the truth
Myth

Impossible. Hypnosis is a natural state of focused attention — like being absorbed in a good book. If the therapist stopped talking, you'd simply drift back to full alertness on your own.

"Only weak-minded people can be hypnotized."
Tap for the truth
Myth

The opposite. Hypnosis is a skill of focus and imagination — people with strong concentration often respond best. It's a collaboration, not something done to you.

"It's just a placebo."
Tap for the truth
Myth

Clinical hypnosis is recognized by major medical bodies and studied in pain, anxiety, IBS, and smoking cessation. Brain imaging shows measurably different activity in hypnosis.
