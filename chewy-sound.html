What people fear about clinical hypnotherapy

Tap a card. Most misconceptions come from stage entertainment, not evidence-based clinical work. Let Meera demystify what's really happening.

"I'll lose control."
Tap for the truth
Myth

You stay fully aware and in control the whole time. Your subconscious mind is always there to protect you — it will never follow any suggestion that conflicts with your values or ethics. Meera is a guide, never a puppeteer.

"I could get stuck in hypnosis."
Tap for the truth
Myth

Impossible. Clinical hypnotherapy is a natural state of focused attention — like being absorbed in a good book. If Meera stopped speaking, you'd naturally drift back to full alertness on your own. Your mind won't let you stay in a state that feels unsafe.

"Only weak-minded people can be hypnotized."
Tap for the truth
Myth

The opposite. Clinical hypnotherapy is a skill of focus and imagination — people with strong concentration and creativity often respond best. It's a true collaboration between you and Meera, not something done to you.

"It's just a placebo."
Tap for the truth
Fact: it's real

Clinical hypnotherapy is recognized by major medical bodies worldwide and has decades of research supporting its effectiveness for pain, anxiety, IBS, PTSD, and smoking cessation. Brain imaging shows measurably different neural activity during clinical hypnosis.
